How to Play PixelRealms

🌱 Expand

You earn 1 expansion token per hour (max 24 stacked) — shown as 🎯 in the top bar. Switch to Expand mode and click an unclaimed tile next to your territory to claim it for 1 token. Water tiles can't be claimed. You can also buy tokens for 1000 gold each.

🗺️ Terrain Overlay

Tap the 🗺️ button (bottom-left, below the zoom buttons) to switch to terrain view. Owned tiles show their underlying terrain color blended with a hint of the nation's color so you can still tell countries apart. Nation borders stay visible. Tap again to switch back to normal view.

⚔️ Attack

Switch to Attack mode and click an enemy tile next to your territory. Your army size vs. their army size (modified by terrain defense — hills and mountains defend better) decides the outcome. Winning captures the tile and damages both armies; losing still costs you troops. Eliminate a nation by taking all their pixels. If the captured tile has a building, there's a 50% chance it transfers to you and a 50% chance it's destroyed.

🏗️ Build

Switch to Build mode and click one of your own tiles to open a building menu. Clicking a tile with an existing building shows a popup where you can delete it (no refund).

⚠️ Building cap: 1 building per 2 pixels. With 10 pixels you can have at most 5 buildings. Expand your territory to unlock more slots — your current usage is shown in My Realm.

💰 Economy

Each pixel earns 1 💰/hr base. Mines and farms add income on top. Your army costs 0.5 💰/hr per unit, and territory costs 0.3 💰/px/hr (each market reduces this by 0.03, max 10 markets). You also pay 10 💰/hr per border pixel — each of your pixels touching an enemy tile adds upkeep, so compact empires are cheaper to defend than sprawling ones. Watch Net gold/hr in My Realm — if it's negative your gold drains, but it will never go below 0.

⚔️ Attacking & War Aggression

Attacking costs your troops no matter the outcome. Each attack (win or lose) raises your army's food consumption rate by +0.05/unit — so a 100-army nation goes from 10 food/hr to 15 food/hr after one attack. This war aggression decays at −0.03/hr base rate (boosted by Markets up to −0.04/hr), so your supply lines recover if you stop fighting. Stocking up food reserves before a war is key — if your stored food drops below 25% of your population, your gold income is penalised by up to −80%. The attack preview shows exactly how your food balance will change before you confirm.

👥 Population & 🌽 Food

Your nation has 20 population per pixel. Population unlocks barracks (100 pop = 5 pixels each). Each pixel produces 0.5 food/hr (foraging) and consumes 0.2 food/hr. Your army consumes 0.1 food/hr per unit at peace — this rises with war aggression (see above). Build farms to cover deficits: each farm produces 5 food/hr, boosted by terrain. If your stored food drops below 25% of your population, your gold income is reduced by up to −80% — so stockpile food before long campaigns. Food is capped at your total population + 20 per farm built. Expand your territory or build farms to raise the cap.

🤝 Trade

Send gold to other nations from the Trade tab — useful for alliances or buying peace.

🗺️ Map

Each nation's territory is outlined in grey with its name shown over its land. Scroll to zoom, drag to pan, and click a nation in the Nations tab to jump to it.
